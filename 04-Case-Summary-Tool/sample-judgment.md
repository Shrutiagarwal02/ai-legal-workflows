**In the matter of:** *State Cooperative Development Corp. v. Municipal Licensing
Authority* (fictional), High Court, Writ Petition No. 000/2026

The Petitioner, a state-linked cooperative development corporation, filed this writ
petition challenging the Respondent Authority's refusal to renew a trade license,
contending that the refusal was issued without a hearing and without recorded reasons,
in violation of principles of natural justice.

The Respondent argued that license renewal is a discretionary administrative function
not subject to a mandatory hearing requirement, and that reasons were communicated
informally to the Petitioner's representative.

The Court held that where a license renewal affects an existing right (as opposed to a
fresh application), the authority is obligated to provide a hearing and record reasons
in writing, even absent an express statutory requirement, as a matter of administrative
fairness. The Court found that informal communication of reasons does not satisfy this
requirement.

The Court allowed the writ petition, quashed the refusal order, and directed the
Respondent Authority to reconsider the renewal application after affording the
Petitioner a hearing and recording written reasons within 30 days.

The Court noted, in passing, that this principle would apply with even greater force
to petitions involving livelihood-affecting licenses, though that question did not
directly arise on the facts of this case.
