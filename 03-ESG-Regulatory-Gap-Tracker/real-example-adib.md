# Real Example — Gap Analysis Run Against ADIB's Public ESG Overview Page

**Source:** Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank (ADIB), public ESG & Sustainability overview page,
[adib.ae/en/esg-and-sustainability](https://www.adib.ae/en/esg-and-sustainability),
retrieved live. This is a genuine, current public disclosure — the bank's own landing
page — not a synthetic example.

**Important context before the analysis:** this is a top-level landing page, not
ADIB's full Sustainability Report (a much longer PDF exists behind it, linked from
this page). Treating a landing page as if it were the complete disclosure would be
unfair to the bank — many "gaps" below likely exist as more detailed content
elsewhere on ADIB's site or in their full report. This is flagged explicitly below,
the same way the YC SAFE example flagged template placeholders rather than treating
them as drafting defects — the discipline is to be honest about what was actually
reviewed, not to overstate a finding.

**What was reviewed (verbatim extract):**
> "As a leading Islamic bank, our commitment to a sustainable future is now embedded
> in our 5-year strategy and plan." The page describes a "Sustainable Future" pillar
> within corporate strategy, a dedicated ESG strategy, and four sub-sections: Our
> Commitments, Policies/Statements & Disclosures, ESG Reports, and ESG Key Figures.
> It states that "reporting and transparent communication with stakeholders... are
> essential components of its sustainability strategy."

---

## Gap Analysis Output

**Item:** Governance
**Status:** Partial
**Evidence found:** References an "ESG strategy" and a "Sustainable Future" pillar within corporate strategy; a "Policies, Statements & Disclosures" section is referenced but its content wasn't reviewed here.
**Gap:** No named governance body, responsible officer, or board committee is stated on this page. (Caveat: this may exist in the linked Policies/Disclosures section, not reviewed.)
**Priority to close:** Medium — likely a landing-page omission rather than an actual gap; verify in the linked disclosures before treating as a real finding.

---

**Item:** Climate/Environmental
**Status:** Missing (on this page)
**Evidence found:** None — no emissions data, climate risk language, or environmental policy appears on the overview page itself.
**Gap:** No visible Scope 1/2/3 disclosure or climate risk statement at this level.
**Priority to close:** High to verify — but likely exists in the "ESG Reports" / "ESG Key Figures" sections linked from this page, not reviewed here.

**Item:** Social
**Status:** Missing (on this page)
**Evidence found:** None on this page.
**Gap:** No workforce, community, or labor-standards content visible at the landing-page level.
**Priority to close:** High to verify in linked sections.

## Overall Readiness Summary

This landing page is a navigation layer, not a disclosure in itself — it names the
right categories (commitments, disclosures, reports, key figures) but the actual
substance sits behind links not reviewed here. **The correct output of this exercise
is not "ADIB has six ESG gaps" — it's "this page cannot be assessed as a complete
disclosure; the underlying Sustainability Report PDF and linked disclosure pages must
be reviewed before drawing conclusions."**

## Why this is the most useful real example in the portfolio, specifically because it's imperfect

This result is more valuable for an interview than a clean pass/fail would be: it
shows the tool (and the person using it) correctly recognizing the difference between
"this document has a gap" and "this document is the wrong scope to assess a gap
from." An AI tool that confidently declared ADIB non-compliant based on a two-paragraph
landing page would be actively misleading — and catching that distinction, rather than
generating six alarming red flags from thin material, is the actual judgment call a
lawyer brings to this kind of tool.
